Research Methodology

  1. To increase the investment appeal, credibility and performance of commercial real estate by creating a quality process to establish current market data that is:

    1. National and Consistent
    2. Accurate and Timely
    3. Credible, Transparent and Independent

  2. To accelerate the differentiation of commercial real estate offerings and services by establishing credible criteria and processes that identify, measure, improve, recognize and reward the performance of buildings, owners, managers, occupants and their advisors, suppliers and service providers.

  3. To meet the evolving market information and relative performance data needs of key industry participants; including building owners, managers, lenders, occupants and their advisors, suppliers and service providers.

  4. To provide key industry participants with the data and tools needed to increase personal, corporate and industry wide performance and to increase the perceived value, cost effectiveness and uniqueness of their offerings and services.

  5. To continue to be recognized as the industry’s pre-eminent and preferred supplier of market and relative performance data and to be respected and supported by commercial real estate owners, managers, occupants, advisors, suppliers, service providers and industry associations.

  6. To improve the quality and value of commercial real estate market forecasts and advisory services by enabling advisors / brokers and their agents to place greater focus on future forecasts / recommendations / advice and on the ‘quality of fit’ between occupant needs and their differentiated facility options.

  7. To create an environment where performance based compensation, which is based upon credible criteria and processes, contributes to the attraction, retention and growth of high performance personnel to commercial real estate owners, managers, lenders, occupants and their advisors, suppliers and service providers.

Barometers

Product/Market Barometer

We have developed a unique barometer to identify the momentum of various product/market combinations across the major Canadian urban centres. Survey participants are asked whether they are a buyer or seller (“hold” not an option), and thus a bullish or bearish response is obtained for each property type/market. We have also included publicly traded real estate corporate stock and REIT units since these instruments compete for capital from the same sources.

Office Vacancy Barometer

We have developed this barometer to identify vacancy momentum for Downtown Class “AA” and Class “B”, as well as Suburban Class “A” and Class “B” buildings for all seven markets. All contributors are asked if vacancy rates will increase, decrease, or stay the same, over the next three months.

The momentum ratio is defined as the largest % directional response divided by the balance of all other % responses. For example, say 75% of respondents believe Montreal Suburban ‘A’ vacancy will decrease, 17% said it would be stable, and 8% said increase. The momentum ratio would equal [75% divided by (17% + 8%)] = 3.0

Investor Outlook

Contributors are asked to comment on expected value trends for each product type over the next 12 months. The result is a forward-looking value expectation.
